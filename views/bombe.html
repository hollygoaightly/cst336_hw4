The bombe is an electro-mechanical computing device used during World War II by Brittish cryptologists to decipher encrypted messages intercepted from the Germans.

The computing device was primarily operated by women, since the tedious work of computing and calculating was considered "women's work".

While women's efforts in the war recieved media coverage at the time, history has largely forgotten their role.

By the year 1943, almost all people employed to work with computers were women.
